Banana chips, with origins in Kerala, India are dried, generally crispy slices of bananas. They can be covered with sugar or honey and have a sweet taste, or are more commonly fried in oil and spices and have a salty or spicy taste.

Kue Lapis
  • by Achmad Zidan
  • 16 Oct, 2020
+

Kue lapis or Kuih Lapis is a traditional snack of steamed colourful layered soft rice flour pudding. In Malay and Indonesian lapis means "layers". This steamed layered sticky rice cake or pudding is quite popular in Malaysia and Indonesia, and also can be found in the Netherlands through their colonial links.

Pecel Telo
  • by Achmad Zidan
  • 16 Oct, 2020
+

Lumajang Telo pecel is made from vegetables such as banana heart, clover, genjer, long beans, and sprouts. Something that makes this food unique is the marinade which is made from sweet potatoes, while in other pecel the seasoning is made from peanuts. This one food is worth a try Friend Traveler.

Latok
  • by Achmad Zidan
  • 16 Oct, 2020
+

Latok cake is not actually the original food from Lumajang but this snack can be found in the area and has even become a favorite snack there. Besides having an attractive color, this cake also tastes very tasty because it is served with a sprinkling of grated coconut.

Bledus
  • by Achmad Zidan
  • 16 Oct, 2020
+

Bledus is one of the typical Lumajang foods that is still in demand today. The ingredients for this bledus are made from dry shelled corn which is soaked overnight, then boiled until the texture becomes more tender. This dry shelled corn is soft because of the long boiling time. To make it even more delicious, don't forget to add grated coconut on top. The colors are bright and attractive.
